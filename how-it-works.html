Fast answer

CoachMVP is a focused setup service, not a long course. The goal is to help you pick one lane, launch one usable asset, and validate the idea fast. Once the site is live, you are responsible for traffic, lead handling, follow-up, outreach, compliance, and monetization.

Step by step

Here is the actual flow after you pay. No mystery. No giant course portal.

  1. 01

    You pay and we get on a call

    Once you pay the founding rate, we book a Google Meet or FaceTime call. I only take on a few candidates at a time, so this starts with a real conversation about your background, your time, and what you want to test.

    • Kickoff call with me directly
    • Limited spots
    • Built for people ready to move

  2. 02

    We narrow you down to one car lane

    We do not start broad. We pick one niche to focus on: one brand, one model family, or one clear seller-intent lane. That keeps the offer simple and gives you a better shot at validating something real.

    • One niche, not ten
    • Built for clarity
    • Good for fast testing

  3. 03

    I research demand and search intent

    I use tools like Google Keyword Planner and SEMrush to look at how people actually search. For example, if someone wants to focus on Porsche owners, I look for seller intent around phrases like sell my Porsche and related searches to see if there is a real angle to build around.

    • Keyword research
    • Demand and intent review
    • Domain direction with transactional intent

  4. 04

    I build the first version with you

    I write the on-page copy with AI plus editing, build a simple lead-gen page, and set up the contact flow so leads come to you. If it makes sense for the niche, that can include a basic VIN intake or similar capture flow. You leave with the actual files.

    • Simple site, not a huge platform
    • AI-assisted copy, edited by me
    • Lead capture routed to your email

  5. 05

    We launch it

    I build in Codex, upload to GitHub Pages, and can deploy to Vercel if you want that setup. GitHub Pages is enough for version one. If you want Vercel, you cover that cost on your side.

    • GitHub Pages is fine for v1
    • Vercel is optional
    • You keep the files and hosting accounts

  6. 06

    You run the business side

    After launch, you are the one who has to market the site, find traffic, reply to leads, text sellers back, and close business. That can mean outreach, search, and platforms like Facebook, Reddit, Craigslist, or other channels that make sense for your niche and your market.

    • You handle the leads
    • You handle seller follow-up
    • You decide how you monetize

  7. 07

    The goal is validation first

    The goal is not to build a giant company on day one. The goal is to get something live, get real feedback, and try to land the first qualified lead or first sale as fast as possible. Once there is traction, then you decide what to improve next.

    • Validation over perfection
    • First sale matters most
    • Then improve the system
